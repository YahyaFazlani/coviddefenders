Advantages and disadvantages of the COVID-19 vaccine

COVID Vaccination

Vaccination is getting rapidly developed and all the steps are taken to ensure their safety. The vaccination is getting developed by using science. Vaccination is not experimental but has gone through all the requirement stages in a clinic.They are effective as it helps you to stop getting and spreading the virus.

There are four types of vaccine in India - Covishield, Covaxin and Sputnik V. They are found in almost all hospitals in India and they are found to be 88% effective.

Advantages

The vaccine is known to have a number of advantages. The most obvious of which is that it will increase your immunity toward COVID-19 but that's not it. If you get infected with COVID-19, the vaccine may prevent you from getting seriously ill. And it also helps prevent the spread of COVID-19 as the antibodies developed may neutralize the virus, if you have it, so you will not pass it on to others.

Disadvantages

The vaccine also has a few side effects such as fever, fatigue, muscle pain, etc. but these are not as serious for most people.

People who shouldn't take the vaccine

The following people should avoid the vaccine:

  • People with a history of severe allergic reaction to any component of the vaccine should not take it
  • The vaccine is not recommended for persons younger than 18 years of age, pending the results of further studies.
  • While pregnancy puts women at higher risk of severe COVID-19, very little data are available to assess vaccine safety in pregnancy.
